# Turning Points in Star Atlas History

Star Atlas did not develop as a straight march from announcement to finished world. Its history is better understood through moments when the project's practical direction changed: an asset market became an operating product, an emissions loop put ships to work, an Unreal client became publicly accessible, a major financial shock narrowed the available runway, on-chain experiments became sustained strategy play, and governance moved from theory into repeated public decisions.

This chronology selects turning points because they changed what players could do, how the project organized itself, or what the community expected. It does not treat every announcement as an event of equal importance.

## 2021: from public vision to markets and fleet utility

The Galactic Marketplace's official operational-release claim on 2021-08-04 created the first durable commercial surface preserved in the Archive. The September Galactic Asset Offering and ATLAS/POLIS sales connected ships, tokens, and markets to the project's public identity. In December, SCORE/Faction Fleet introduced the first released on-chain gameplay loop, allowing ships to be enlisted for ATLAS rewards. [Product Timeline](Product-Timeline.md)

This period established several patterns that would recur: assets could exist before their full future utility; official publications often combined present releases with a much larger vision; and economic participation became part of the player experience well before the wider strategy and Unreal products matured.

## 2022: governance, Unreal access, and the FTX shock

The DAO and POLIS-locking release in July made governance operational before the later PIP framework formally organized the constitutional roles. The Showroom's September pre-alpha release on the Epic Games Store gave players a public Unreal Engine environment. Breakpoint in November renamed Project S.C.R.E.A.M. as SAGE and presented a wide product roadmap. Each event was significant for a different reason: governance introduced institutional participation, the Showroom introduced direct 3D access, and Breakpoint reorganized the product vision.

Days after Breakpoint, FTX collapsed. Hologram News Network reported on 2022-11-15 that Michael Wagner had disclosed that approximately half of ATMTA's liquid cash reserves were held at FTX. The same contemporary report described community efforts to support the project and noted uncertainty around financing. This is important contemporary reporting, but the Archive does not possess audited financial statements or the complete primary disclosure needed to independently establish the exact exposure and recovery. ## 2023: experimentation, formal governance, and contraction

Escape Velocity's limited public alpha tested real-time on-chain movement beginning in April. Showroom R2.1 expanded the Unreal module in June. In September, SAGE Labs created a persistent browser strategy loop around mining, crafting, transport, and trade. Together, these releases moved Star Atlas from asset utility and demonstrations toward several playable product forms.

The institutional environment was changing at the same time. PIP-1 through PIP-3 formalized the DAO, Foundation, and Council framework. Yet 2023 also included a major reported contraction. On 2023-07-26, HNN reported layoffs, founder pay cuts, and a reprioritization toward SAGE and a mobile application, with several other surfaces receiving reduced attention. Exact staffing numbers and runway remain unresolved because the surviving record is contemporary community reporting rather than a complete corporate filing. [SRC-HNN-A0BE0ADE1E7A46D5](../../archive/source-records/campaign-charlie-hnn/SRC-HNN-A0BE0ADE1E7A46D5.md)

The turning point was not simply "layoffs occurred." It was that product sequencing, institutional capacity, and community expectations were forced to adjust together.

## 2024: Starbased and constitutional voting

The Starbased update changed the SAGE Labs economy around shared starbases, Loyalty Points, Council Rank XP, epochs, and revised emissions. Its launch was delayed during program deployment and then stated live on 2024-04-04 with important first-epoch limitations. At roughly the same time, the end of SCORE/Faction Fleet emissions was announced as part of a wider transition, although the repository still lacks an execution record proving the exact terminal reward state.

Governance also became more legible through recurring PIP votes and Council operations. The historical importance lies less in the number of proposals than in the emergence of recognizable distinctions among proposal, vote, passage, authorization, implementation, payment, cancellation, and withdrawal.

## 2025: community programs and simulation

Governance increasingly authorized community-facing programs, creator work, infrastructure, events, and ecosystem funding. These votes demonstrate institutional activity, but they do not by themselves prove payments, completion, or outcomes. The Ecosystem Fund's refresh under PIP-23 also showed how policy could supersede an earlier authorization without erasing its history.

Holosim provided a different kind of turning point: a browser simulation could iterate fleets, seasons, progression, and later faction systems without being treated as the same release state as SAGE or a mainnet product. Its history made build-level qualification essential.

## 2026: PTR systems, Chapter 2, and unresolved execution

Official support material documented C4 Public Test Realm mechanics in 2026. PTR access establishes testing, not general release. Holosim Chapter 2 likewise has a well-preserved sequence of plan, delay, reschedule, and live statement, demonstrating why dates and states must be attached to a named build.

PIP-33, the ATMTA Historic Expense Reimbursement proposal, passed in July 2026. Passage established authorization under the captured governance record. Payment and on-chain execution remain unverified, preserving another recurring lesson: even a consequential vote is not the end of the historical chain.

## Continuity beneath the turning points

Several themes cross all periods:

- Star Atlas has repeatedly developed through parallel product surfaces rather than one client.
- Economic programs often change before every historical interface or program disappears.
- Community media preserves crises and reactions that official channels may not document in comparable detail.
- Governance produces several states after a vote, and later evidence can change an implementation assessment without changing the vote result.
- The project's public ambition has consistently exceeded the set of features that can be documented as released at any one time.
