# Krigs

Krigs—also rendered **Kr1gs** in parts of the historical record—is a journalist, publisher, and community figure best documented as the founder and operator of the Hologram News Network. His work matters both as a body of reporting and as firsthand evidence about the operation of a major Star Atlas community publication.

## HNN and the historical record

The audited HNN corpus contains original reporting, interviews, editorials, features, event coverage, reference material, and investigative work. It is preserved as a community primary source rather than flattened into summaries. The earliest preserved article establishes the beginning of the archive's surviving written HNN record, not necessarily the network's founding date.

Krigs's institutional role is well supported by the campaign. That does not mean every unbylined article was personally written by him. Ownership, operation, editorship, authorship, and a publication's institutional voice remain distinct.

## Publication identities that must remain separate

The repository preserves several related identities:

1. **Hologram News Network / HNN** — the news organization and its publication record.
2. **The Hologram** — a distinct Medium publication identity.
3. **`thehologram.io` and other archived HNN websites** — historical web surfaces whose URLs and capture histories remain provenance.
4. **Krigs's personal Medium or creator identity** — personal publishing that does not automatically become an HNN publication.

These surfaces may share an operator or author, but they are not interchangeable. HNN material must never be attributed to Intergalactic Herald, and a later mirror should not erase the original publication identity.

## How to use Krigs as a source

Surviving contemporaneous publications are documentary evidence. A signed article can establish authorship; an HNN masthead or publication record can establish institutional publication; and an attributed interview can establish what a participant said. Each claim still carries the article's date, source lineage, and publication role.

Krigs is also a first-party source for HNN operations and for events he personally experienced. Retrospective recollections should be labelled as attributed firsthand evidence until corroborated. They are valuable, but they are not silently converted into contemporaneous documentation.

## Source-critical limitations

Community journalism can preserve events that official sources omitted, precede a later official announcement, or add essential local context. It can also rely on incomplete information. The archive therefore preserves HNN's original framing while reconciliation records distinguish original reporting, interviews, references, and later confirmation or conflict.

Missing bylines remain unknown. Publication ownership does not fill them in. A recollection does not rewrite an older article, and later domain changes do not change the original canonical URL or publisher identity.
