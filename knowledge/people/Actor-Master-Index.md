## Roles are time-bound

A person may hold several roles, and none should be silently converted into another. Founder, executive, employee, Foundation officer, Council member, proposal author, guild leader, guild member, journalist, moderator, creator, and event host are distinct states. A historical title is not current merely because it appears in an old announcement.

For elected governance roles, a candidate advancing from one ballot stage is not a final winner. Election establishes a term or office only to the extent supported by the result and later seating evidence. For community roles, a display tag or third-party mention can support association without establishing formal leadership.

## Transcript attribution and informational value

The Atlas Brew corpus often labels speakers as `UNKNOWN`. This limits person-specific quotation and authority claims, but it does not erase the historical value of the discussion. Significant information can still be indexed when the exact recording, source ID, segment, timestamp, and supporting captions are preserved.

Speaker identity becomes essential when a claim depends on personal or institutional authority—for example, attributing an official commitment to Michael Wagner. In those cases, unknown speaker status prevents named attribution unless separate evidence resolves it. For general product or community history, a timestamped unknown-speaker segment may remain a useful lead or corroborating record.

## Discord context

Discord evidence is evaluated for Star Atlas significance, not message volume alone. It can preserve institutional announcements, product explanations, community projects, guild relationships, and sustained Star Atlas-relevant disputes. Unrelated politics, culture-war discussion, unrelated games, and off-topic personal attacks are excluded from knowledge evaluation even though raw preservation remains intact.

## Profile standard

A mature person profile should include aliases, dated roles, organizational affiliations, public statements by subject area, governance participation, media appearances, conflicts of interest, uncertainties, and the evidence that connects each claim. Reputation and controversy require attributable sources and cannot be reduced to a character judgment.

## Priority research

The most valuable next artifacts are dated leadership rosters and role-change announcements; later Council winner and seating records; public self-identification for pseudonymous figures; original guild leadership notices; and speaker-labelled recordings for the relatively small subset of claims where personal authority materially changes interpretation.
