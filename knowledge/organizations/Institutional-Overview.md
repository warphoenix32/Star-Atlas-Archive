# Star Atlas Institutional Overview

Star Atlas is not governed or operated by a single undifferentiated actor. Its public record contains a company, a token-weighted governance system, a legal and administrative foundation, an elected council, and bounded treasury programs. Their responsibilities overlap, but their identities and authority do not.

## The institutional map

| Institution | Principal role in the captured record | Boundary that must remain visible |
|---|---|---|
| [ATMTA, Inc.](ATMTA.md) | Develops and operates Star Atlas products and publishes official company communications | Not the DAO electorate and not proof that every announced feature was delivered |
| [Star Atlas DAO](../governance/Star-Atlas-DAO.md) | POLIS-locking, PVP-weighted governance system through which eligible holders consider proposals | A passed vote authorizes policy; it does not itself execute code, transfer funds, or prove completion |
| [Star Atlas Foundation](../governance/Star-Atlas-Foundation.md) | Legal and administrative implementation body described in the captured framework | Administrative and safety discretion is not separate legislative sovereignty |
| [Star Atlas Council](../governance/Star-Atlas-Council.md) | Elected governance-process steward, author-support body, and delegated program administrator | Council-authored tracker assessments are not independent verification |
| [Ecosystem Fund](../governance/Ecosystem-Fund.md) | Bounded governance policy instrument and funding mechanism | Not the whole treasury architecture and not an independent organization |

## How authority moves

A governance action ordinarily begins with an author and a proposal. Eligible voters may then vote through the DAO mechanism. A recorded result can establish passage or failure, but passage must remain separate from later authorization language, administrative action, implementation, payment, and verified outcome.

The Foundation administers the legal and procedural framework described in the captured PIPs. The Council can assist authors, operate governance processes, and perform delegated program work. ATMTA may develop the affected product or be a proposal party. These institutions can participate in the same sequence without becoming the same legal or canonical entity.

## Treasury is an architecture, not one actor

The [treasury architecture](../economy/DAO-Treasury-Architecture.md) includes distinct accounts, programs, authorization paths, and institutional responsibilities. The named **DAO Treasury account** is one specific account. It must not be merged with lockers, emissions, token-sale mechanisms, the Ecosystem Fund wallet, or every asset associated with DAO policy.

Similarly, an approved expenditure remains only an authorization until payment evidence is available. Council reporting can document an institutional assessment, but on-chain verification requires a separate dataset and method.

## Why the distinctions matter

Collapsing these institutions produces predictable historical errors: treating a company announcement as a DAO decision, a vote as implementation, a Council assessment as independent proof, or a Foundation action as the act of every POLIS holder. The archive instead records who spoke, which mechanism acted, what state the action reached, and which later evidence remains missing.

## Current-state caution

As of 2026-07-20, the strongest institutional baseline is the captured PIP corpus, official publication archive, and reviewed governance ledger. Current officeholders, signers, delegations, and corporate roles may differ from historical records. A title or role is current only when a dated source supports that status.
